Fire Season

As part of my master's thesis, I would like to create a D3 visualization charting the beginning of fire season over the past 50 years.

In general, fire season has started sooner each year and has gone on longer, says Tony Westerling, a climate change and wildfire expert at UC Merced. The trend correlates with years of warm springs and early snow melts, Westerling says.

The fire season for a given year would be represented by horizontal bars. Additional layers could be toggled on and off to show the year's snow melt and average temperature.
